Privacy Policy

Last updated: January 2025

Liberator is designed with privacy at its core. We believe your AI conversations belong to you, and only you. This policy explains how the extension handles your data, which is simple: your data stays on your device.

Data Collection

We collect nothing. Liberator does not send any data to external servers. There is no analytics, no tracking, no telemetry.

All conversation data captured by the extension is stored locally in your browser's IndexedDB storage. This data never leaves your device unless you explicitly export it.

What We Access

Liberator requires access to the following AI chat websites to read your conversation history:

  • - ChatGPT (chatgpt.com, chat.openai.com)
  • - Claude (claude.ai)
  • - Gemini (gemini.google.com)
  • - Grok (grok.com, x.com)
  • - Copilot (copilot.microsoft.com)

This access is used solely to read and store your conversations locally. We do not modify, delete, or interact with these sites beyond reading publicly visible conversation data from your logged-in sessions.

Local Storage

All data is stored in your browser's local IndexedDB database. This includes:

  • - Conversation threads and messages
  • - AI-generated summaries (processed locally)
  • - Vector embeddings for similarity search
  • - Your settings and preferences

You can delete all stored data at any time through the extension's options page or by removing the extension from your browser.

Third Parties

Liberator does not share, sell, or transfer your data to any third parties. There are no advertising partners, analytics providers, or data brokers involved.

If you choose to use optional features like OpenAI embeddings, those API calls are made directly from your browser using your own API key. We never see or store your API keys on any server.

Open Source

Liberator is open source. You can audit the code yourself to verify these privacy claims. We believe transparency is essential for trust.

Contact

If you have questions about this privacy policy or how Liberator handles your data, please open an issue on our GitHub repository.
